1. GAME OVERVIEW
The world has fallen to mutated boars whose Ferocity (R), Cunning (B), and Mass (G) threaten the last survivors.
 Each player builds a personal engine using stances, upgrades, weapons, and tokens, then fights through two armies‚ÄîDay and Night‚Äîeach culminating in a Boss with layered damage thresholds.
Players generate resources each round depending on their stance, use them to defeat threats, upgrade their character, extend slots, or reposition strategically.
The winner is the survivor with the most Victory Points (VP) from threats, upgrades, boss tokens, and bonuses.

6. ROUND STRUCTURE
Each round, players take turns clockwise.
a. Start-of-Round Phase
Each player:
Produces resources from their stance
Gains any per-round upgrade income
Tokens and unspent resources DO NOT expire unless a card says so.

b. Player Turn (Choose ONE Action)
ACTION A ‚Äî Fight a Threat
You may fight a threat only if you can fully pay its cost using:
Resources from production
Tokens
Weapon effects
Mass token reductions
Stance discount (‚àí1)

You may only attack:
The front threat in any of the three rows
Second-row threats only if a weapon/ability allows (e.g., Snipe Scope)

If defeated:
Gain VP equal to threat‚Äôs VP value
Gain its spoil reward (token or consumable)
Move it from the row to your spoils zone. If a threat has a "CONSUME:" ability,
you can consume it any time to activate its effect (discard from spoils zone after resolving effect).
Slide the column downward to align first threat of that column
to first threats of other columns

ACTION B ‚Äî Buy an Upgrade
Pay the upgrade cost in resources.
Place it in an available Upgrade Slot.
Upgrades grant VP when purchased and permanent effects.
If no slots are available, this action cannot be performed.

ACTION C ‚Äî Extend a Slot
Choose either:
+1 Upgrade Slot OR
+1 Weapon Slot

Then gain 1 Wild Token.
Max slots: 4 each.
If you have maxed all slots, this action cannot be performed.

ACTION D ‚Äî Realign
Move your stance to ANY stance, bypassing movement rules.
Gain 1 Wild Token.
This action is your tempo reset and strategic repositioning tool.
If you are in a stance from which you can move to any other stance in one step
(i.e., following the rules), you cannot perform this action.

ACTION E - Scavenge
Gain 1 Wild Token

c. End turn
Turn passes to next player clockwise.
When all players played a turn in a round, initiative token moves counter-clockwise.
This means that the last player in first round becomes the first player in next round
(effectively playing two turns in a row).
